## 1. Identity

**Full name:** Meru Teaching and Referral Hospital
**Short name / brand:** MeTRH
**Type:** Public referral hospital, medical training and research institution
**Facility level:** KEPH Level 6 (Specialized & Tertiary Referral Hospital) — per KMHFR, the hospital has been elevated from Level 5 to Level 6 status
**Ownership:** Ministry of Health (Kenya) — public facility, county-affiliated (Meru County)
**Date established:** 17 March 2016 (per KMHFR regulatory record; hospital itself traces its lineage back further, having "transitioned from a dispensary" over the years)
**Regulatory status:** Regulated, pending gazettement; KHIS reporting yes; NHIF/SHA accredited; open 24 hours, 7 days a week, including late night

**Tagline (from previous site):** "Exemplary Health Care for You"

**One-line positioning:** MeTRH is the principal referral hospital for Upper Eastern Kenya — public, affordable, specialized, and a teaching/research institution in one.

## 9. Institutional Role & Teaching Affiliations

MeTRH serves as the teaching hospital for:

- Kenya Methodist University (KeMU)
- Meru University of Science and Technology

It is an internship centre for Ministry of Health interns, clinical officers, nurses, nutritionists, and hosts research activity (e.g., published studies on antimicrobial resistance conducted at the hospital with Kenyatta University ethics approval).

MeTRH participates in county, national, and global healthcare policy formulation and positions itself as playing a critical role in national/regional/county health agenda delivery.

## 10. Growth, Development & Milestones

**Historical trajectory:** MeTRH transitioned from a dispensary → to a Level 5 hospital → and is now transitioning to Level 6 (specialized referral) status.

**Key achievements/capabilities acquired over time:**

- MRI and CT scan
- Advanced laboratory testing incl. tumour markers / GeneXpert, pathology department
- Dialysis unit
- Oxygen plant
- 1000 KVA backup generators (power redundancy)
- NHIF (now SHA) wing
- Trauma ward
- Ambulatory services
- Specialty clinics: Oncology, Palliative Care, Maxillofacial, ENT
- Improved mortuary services
- Comprehensive rehabilitation services
- Cleft lip and palate surgical camps
- Laparoscopy / minimal access surgery

**Current major projects:**

- **New 250-bed ward block** — under construction (per legacy site "Growth and Development" section)
- **Dedicated Cancer Centre** — a Public-Private Partnership (PPP) project registered with Kenya's PPP Directorate: a 50-bed capacity facility with anatomic pathology and medical imaging capacity, offering chemotherapy, radiotherapy, and surgery as treatment options. This is a significant capital project worth featuring prominently.
- **Level 6 transition** — hospital-wide departmental changes as MeTRH becomes a fully-fledged Level 6 facility, expected to improve patient care levels and specialized service delivery (per Board Chairman Prof. Jotham Micheni and Director Dr. Joseph Wahome).

**2025 milestone — First spinal/neurosurgery in the region:**
In 2023, a team led by newly-hired neurosurgeon Dr. Muthoka Mativo performed MeTRH's (and the Upper Eastern region's) first spinal surgeries — two operations including a neck fracture case and a paralysis case. This meant patients with spinal, neck, and head injuries no longer needed referral to Nairobi. Then-CEO Dr. Gacheri Kathiiri framed this as central to the hospital's transformation into a specialized facility, alongside a "one-bed-per-patient" policy and a shift toward referral-only admissions (phasing out walk-ins), with level-four hospitals in the region gaining their own specialists to reduce pressure on MeTRH.

**2025 milestone — Oesophageal cancer screening partnership:**
MeTRH partnered with the National Cancer Institute of Kenya and Meru County (mobile screening unit, endoscopy across all sub-counties) to improve early detection of oesophageal cancer. MeTRH committed to maintaining weekly endoscopy services every Wednesday as an ongoing service. Stakeholders present included County Finance & Economic Planning CECM CPA Monica Kathono, KUTRRH's Dr. Caroline Ngugi, MeTRH's then-Ag. CEO Dr. Bernard Murithi, and County Director of Health Dr. Koome Muthuri.

**Renal unit:** Featured by the hospital as a hope-and-healing story — "Meru County's renal unit at MeTRH continues to transform lives" (dialysis services, ongoing patient impact storytelling — good source of a patient-story/testimonial content type).

**Corporate Clinic:** MeTRH operates a Corporate Clinic (referenced as "now fully operational" in FB posts) — likely a private/corporate-facing wing or occupational health service. Worth a dedicated service page; confirm exact scope with client.

**Oncology Centre — linear accelerator/equipment:** FB references to the Oncology Centre being "equipped with two acc[elerators]" — likely referring to radiotherapy equipment (LINACs) as part of the Cancer Centre buildout. Confirm exact equipment list with client before publishing specifics.

## 11. Community Engagement & Blood Donation Drives

Blood donation is a recurring, high-visibility community engagement theme for MeTRH — strong candidate for a "News & Community" or "Get Involved" section on the site.

- **Manchester United Fans Kenya (Meru chapter):** ~100 fans donated blood at MeTRH in a organized drive (covered by Nation Media/K24).
- **Arsenal Kenya Supporters' Club (AKSC):** Organized a large blood donation drive in Meru town (2026), collecting 300+ units, timed around an Arsenal vs. Newcastle Premier League match — framed as sports-fan-club-led social impact. Public health experts at "Meru Level 5 Hospital" (note: some coverage still uses the old "Level 5" name) noted donated blood is critical for cancer patients, children with severe anaemia, and women during childbirth, with demand spiking during rainy season due to road accidents.
- **County-led mass blood donation drives:** Meru County Government (in collaboration with Kenya Red Cross) has run mass blood donation drives at county HQ, with the Governor publicly urging residents to donate — citing real cases of lives saved through transfusion at MeTRH's blood transfusion satellite centre (a child, a mother with complicated delivery, a boda boda accident victim).
- **Regional context:** Kenya faces a chronic blood shortage nationally (Kenya National Blood Transfusion Service collects under 200,000 units annually against a 500,000-unit national requirement); Meru's situation is compounded by high accident rates on the Meru–Nanyuki highway and maternal complications.
